## 0. How to Read This

**Three categories** — the "supporting papers" for each boundary entry are divided as follows.

| Category | Meaning | cdGTS counterpart |
|---|---|---|
| **(1) Defining data** | The marker that physically fixes the boundary — fossil FAD, geomagnetic reversal, isotope anomaly | data node · correlation event |
| **(2) age model** | The basis for the boundary age — U-Pb·Ar-Ar radiometric dating, astrochronology | process node · age determination |
| **(3) ratification** | Official IUGS/ICS confirmation announcement (usually *Episodes*) | release node · confirmed release |

**Notation for definition method**
- **GSSP** — defined by an actual outcrop "golden spike" (the Phanerozoic standard).
- **GSSA** — defined by an agreed numerical age with no outcrop (the Precambrian standard).
- **pending / unratified** — a candidate section and marker exist but ratification has not occurred.
- **redefinition/revision** — already ratified, but re-designation is under way due to submergence or marker problems.

## 3. Boundary-by-Boundary Detail

### Precambrian

> The Precambrian (ca. 4.567 Ga–539 Ma) accounts for roughly 88% of geologic time, but its method of definition is fundamentally different from that of the Phanerozoic. The overwhelming majority of boundaries are set as **GSSAs (numerical, chronometric definitions)** rather than actual outcrops, and rock-based GSSPs are the exception. For a long time the base of the Ediacaran (~635 Ma) was the only rock-based Precambrian GSSP, but with the successive ratification of the base of the Eoarchean (Acasta Gneiss) in 2023 and the base of the Hadean (meteorite GSSA) in 2024, the lowermost part of geologic time was formally defined for the first time. At the same time, a reform effort — led by Shields et al. (2022) — to convert all Precambrian GSSAs to rock-based definitions is under way.

#### Hadean base — 4567.30 ± 0.16 Ma · GSSA
- **Definition:** The age of the oldest solid material of the Solar System's protoplanetary disk. It uses U-corrected Pb-Pb ages of the CAIs (calcium-aluminium inclusions) of meteorites such as Allende and Gujba as the anchor. Because it is a chronometric definition anchored to meteorite ages rather than to an actual terrestrial outcrop, it was ratified as a **GSSA** rather than a GSSP (the ratification paper's title explicitly designates it a "GSSA").
- **Ratification/basis:** Halla, Noffke, Reis, Awramik et al. (2024) "Ratification of the base of the ICS Geological Time Scale: the Global Standard Stratigraphic Age (GSSA) for the Hadean lower boundary," *Episodes* 47(2):381–389.
- **Discussion:** This is a very recent (2024) measure that, for the first time, formally pins the "floor" of all of geologic time to an official number. Because almost no rock of this age survives on Earth itself (the oldest minerals are Jack Hills zircon fragments ~4.4 Ga), taking the age of Solar System formation as a proxy anchor is unavoidable.

#### Eoarchean base (lowermost Archean) — 4031 ± 3 Ma · GSSP (rock-based)
- **Definition:** The **Acasta Gneiss Complex** of the Slave Province, Northwest Territories, Canada. The marker is the "10 oldest zircon U-Pb ages" of the complex's oldest felsic crust. It is the Archean's first rock-based boundary, but because it is defined by an age cluster rather than a physical "point," GSSP/GSSA notation is used interchangeably in the ICS literature.
- **Ratification/basis:** Ratified by ICS in 2023/09 (chart v2023/09), replacing the former interim 4000 Ma. The formal ratification paper is listed as "*Episodes*, forthcoming" in the ICS GSSP table and is **not yet published** — currently cite ICS chart v2023/09. (The 2024 *Episodes* 47(2):381–389 Halla et al. is the **Hadean** base paper — do not conflate it with this boundary.)
- **Discussion:** Taking the oldest directly dated felsic rock body confirmed on Earth as the boundary carries great symbolic weight, dividing the Hadean (no terrestrial rock) from the Archean (rock record present) by an actual outcrop. Because it is close to a numerical criterion — "oldest zircon age" — rather than a pure GSSP, the fact that it is a rock-anchored definition is a point of nomenclatural debate.

#### Paleoarchean · Mesoarchean · Neoarchean bases — 3600 / 3200 / 2800 Ma · GSSA
- **Definition:** Numerical definitions (round numbers). There is no outcrop-based GSSP; the interior of the Archean is subdivided by convenient rounded ages.
- **Ratification/basis:** ICS/IUGS chronometric definitions. Traditional Precambrian chronometric subdivision schemes such as Plumb (1991).
- **Discussion:** Because these are pure numbers for computational convenience rather than specific geologic events, they are a central target of the Shields et al. (2022) reform. Now that the Eoarchean has moved to a rock-based definition, redefining the remaining Archean-internal boundaries remains an open task.

#### Bases of the Proterozoic eras/periods (Siderian 2500 · Rhyacian 2300 · Orosirian 2050 · Statherian 1800 · Calymmian 1600 · Ectasian 1400 · Stenian 1200 · Tonian 1000 Ma) — GSSA
- **Definition:** All numerical definitions (round numbers). All, including the Tonian base at 1000 Ma, are chronometric GSSAs with no stratigraphic outcrop.
- **Ratification/basis:** ICS/IUGS chronometric definitions.
- **Discussion:** That the entire Proterozoic is defined only by numbers is a representative unfinished point of the current time scale. Shields et al. (2022) proposed replacing these GSSAs with rock-based units anchored to actual geologic events (the Great Oxidation Event, the Lomagundi carbon-isotope excursion, supercontinent cycles), along with new period names such as Skourian and Kleisian, but these are **unratified**.
- **Supporting paper (reform proposal):** Shields et al. (2022) "A template for an improved rock-based subdivision of the pre-Cryogenian timescale," *J. Geol. Soc.* 179(1), jgs2020-222.

#### Cryogenian base — provisionally ~720 Ma · GSSP pending
- **Definition:** Undetermined. The former 850 Ma GSSA has been discarded and provisionally moved to a corrected numerical age of ~720 Ma tied to the onset of the Sturtian glaciation, but an actual rock-based GSSP is **undetermined**.
- **Ratification/basis:** No formal ratification (provisional). Proposals: Shields-Zhou, Porter & Halverson (2016) "A new rock-based definition for the Cryogenian Period (circa 720–635 Ma)," *Episodes* 39(1); Shields et al. (2018) "Descent into the Cryogenian," *Precambrian Research*.
- **Discussion:** A representative case of name-vs-geology mismatch. The Cryogenian is intended to signify global glaciation (Snowball Earth), yet the old 850 Ma GSSA was a number unrelated to any glaciation. Convergence is under way toward aligning the base with the onset of the Sturtian glacial deposits (~717 Ma volcanic U-Pb), but the candidate section and GSSP remain undetermined.

#### Ediacaran base — ~635 Ma · GSSP (rock-based)
- **Definition:** The **Enorama Creek** section in the Flinders Ranges, South Australia. The marker is the **base of the cap carbonate (Nuccaleena Formation)** immediately following the end of the Marinoan glaciation. Until the ratification of the Eoarchean and Hadean, it was the only non-numerical Precambrian GSSP.
- **Ratification/basis:** Knoll, Walter, Narbonne & Christie-Blick (2006) "The Ediacaran Period: a new addition to the geologic time scale," *Lethaia* 39:13–30. (Ratified 2004, described 2006.)
- **Discussion:** An exemplary case of a Precambrian rock-based definition anchored to a distinct global event — the end of the Neoproterozoic Snowball Earth. It is the last period before the Phanerozoic and hosts the first large metazoans (the Ediacara biota).

### Cambrian System

> Of the ten Cambrian stages, six have ratified GSSPs: Fortunian, Wuliuan (former Stage 5, base of the Miaolingian Series), Drumian, Guzhangian, Paibian (base of the Furongian Series), and Jiangshanian. The remaining **Stages 2·3·4·10** are undetermined, leaving the two lower series and the uppermost boundary open. Most of the ratified boundaries are defined by trilobite or agnostid FADs, with only the lowermost defined by a trace fossil.

#### Fortunian base (= base of the Cambrian · base of the Terreneuvian · Precambrian/Cambrian boundary) — 538.8 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Fortune Head, Newfoundland, Canada · FAD of the trace fossil *Treptichnus (Trichophycus) pedum*
- **Ratification:** Brasier, Cowie & Taylor (1994) "Decision on the Precambrian–Cambrian boundary stratotype" *Episodes* 17:95–100.
- **Discussion:** The base of the entire Phanerozoic and the only boundary defined by a trace fossil rather than a body fossil. The physical GSSP point is fixed, but the absolute age was lowered from 541 Ma to **538.8 ± 0.6 Ma** in the 2022 revision. This is based on volcanic-ash U-Pb from Oman and Namibia and correlation with the disappearance of Ediacaran-type fossils (*Cloudina*, *Namacalathus*) and the carbon-isotope decline (BACE).
- **Supporting papers:** *ratification* Brasier et al. (1994) *Episodes* 17:95–100. *age model* Linnemann et al. (2019) "New high-resolution age data from the Ediacaran–Cambrian boundary indicate rapid, ecologically driven onset of the Cambrian explosion," *Terra Nova* 31(1):49–58, doi:10.1111/ter.12368 — CA-ID-TIMS U-Pb from a Namibian composite section constrains the transition to 538.99 ± 0.21 → 538.58 ± 0.19 Ma.

#### Cambrian Stage 2 base — ~529 Ma · pending
- **Definition:** No GSSP. Candidate marker: FAD of the small shelly fossil *Watsonella crosbyi* (with the ZHUCE carbon-isotope event as a secondary marker).
- **Discussion:** Marker selection has been delayed because the provinciality and reworking of SSFs give low reliability for global correlation. A combined proposal of *W. crosbyi* + carbon isotopes in the Yanjiahe Formation of South China and elsewhere is under discussion but not agreed.

#### Cambrian Stage 3 base (= base of Series 2) — ~521 Ma · pending
- **Definition:** No GSSP. Candidate marker: a global trilobite FAD lineage (candidate species and section undetermined).
- **Discussion:** The direction of using the "first appearance of trilobites" as the boundary is agreed upon, but the fact that trilobite FADs are markedly diachronous across continents is the core difficulty.

#### Cambrian Stage 4 base — ~514 Ma · pending
- **Definition:** No GSSP. Candidate marker: a trilobite FAD lineage (specific candidate undetermined).
- **Discussion:** Both stages of Series 2 are undetermined. The top is closed by the base of the Wuliuan, but the bottom is open.

#### Wuliuan base (= former Stage 5 · base of the Miaolingian Series) — ~506.5 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Wuliu-Zengjiayan, Balang, Jianhe, Guizhou, China · FAD of the trilobite *Oryctocephalus indicus*
- **Ratification:** Zhao et al. (2019) *Episodes* 42(3):165–184. (IUGS ratified 2018-06)
- **Discussion:** The bases of the series (Miaolingian) and stage (Wuliuan) coincide (conterminous). *O. indicus* is widely distributed from shallow seas to the outer shelf, favoring global correlation. China's 11th GSSP.

#### Drumian base — ~504.5 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Drum Mountains, Utah, USA · FAD of the agnostid *Ptychagnostus atavus*
- **Ratification:** Babcock et al. (2007) *Episodes* 30:85–95.
- **Discussion:** As a pelagic agnostid it has excellent intercontinental correlation power. The FAD horizon also correlates with a carbon-isotope event (near DICE).

#### Guzhangian base — ~500.5 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Louyixi, Hunan, China · FAD of the agnostid *Lejopyge laevigata*
- **Ratification:** Peng et al. (2009) *Episodes* 32:41–55.
- **Discussion:** A widely distributed agnostid defines the uppermost stage of the Miaolingian. Just above this boundary follows the SPICE event of the Furongian base.

#### Paibian base (= base of the Furongian Series) — ~497 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Paibi, Wuling Mountains, Hunan, China · FAD of the agnostid *Glyptagnostus reticulatus*
- **Ratification:** Peng et al. (2004) *Lethaia* 37:365–379.
- **Discussion:** The FAD of *G. reticulatus* almost coincides with the rising limb of the global positive carbon-isotope anomaly **SPICE** (Steptoean Positive Carbon Isotope Excursion), making it a representative boundary where biostratigraphy and chemostratigraphy are strongly linked.
- **Supporting papers:** *ratification* Peng et al. (2004) *Lethaia* 37:365–379. *data* Pulsipher et al. (2021) "…the SPICEraq database" *Earth-Science Reviews* 212:103442.

#### Jiangshanian base — ~494 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Duibian B, Zhejiang, China · FAD of the agnostid *Agnostotes orientalis*
- **Ratification:** Peng et al. (2012) *Episodes* 35:462–477.
- **Discussion:** A wide-ranging agnostid linking Laurentia, Gondwana, and China, responsible for correlation after the end of SPICE.

#### Cambrian Stage 10 base — ~489.5 Ma · pending
- **Definition:** No GSSP. Candidate markers: FAD of the conodont *Eoconodontus notchpeakensis* or FAD of the trilobite *Lotagnostus americanus*.
- **Discussion:** The uppermost and last undetermined stage of the Cambrian. *L. americanus* has identification controversy and *E. notchpeakensis* has correlation-power problems, so the markers compete. The top is closed by the base of the Tremadocian (Ordovician).

### Ordovician System

> A system with high internal consistency, as all 7 stages are confirmed by GSSP. Markers alternate between graptolite and conodont FADs, and the uppermost Hirnantian is also strongly characterized by a carbon isotope excursion (HICE) associated with mass extinction and continental glaciation.

#### Tremadocian base (= base of the Ordovician) — 486.85 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Green Point, Gros Morne, Newfoundland, Canada · conodont *Iapetognathus fluctivagus* FAD
- **Ratification:** Cooper, Nowlan & Williams (2001) *Episodes* 24:19–28.
- **Discussion:** The Cambrian/Ordovician boundary. The conodont FAD lies slightly below the appearance of the graptolite *Rhabdinopora*. The age was raised from 485.4 to **486.85 Ma** in the 2024/12 revision. However, since 2011 there has been a marker-horizon debate over whether *I. fluctivagus* at Green Point may not be the first appearance of this genus.

#### Floian base — 477.7 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Diabasbrottet, Västergötland, Sweden · graptolite *Tetragraptus approximatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** Bergström et al. (2004) *Episodes* 27:265–272.
- **Discussion:** Defined by a planktonic graptolite FAD, directly tied to the graptolite zonation. Anchored in the standard Balto-Scandian graptolite stratigraphy.

#### Dapingian base — 470.0 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Huanghuachang, Yichang, China · conodont *Baltoniodus triangularis* FAD
- **Ratification:** Wang et al. (2009) "The Global Stratotype Section and Point for the base of the Middle Ordovician Series and the Third Stage (Dapingian)," *Episodes* 32(2):96–113.
- **Discussion:** The Lower/Middle Ordovician boundary. Defined by an evolutionary event in the *Baltoniodus* lineage. The year conflict is resolved: the 2005 *Episodes* 28(2):105–117 was the proposal, IUGS-ratified in 2007, with the definitive GSSP paper in 2009.

#### Darriwilian base — 467.3 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Huangnitang, Zhejiang, China · graptolite *Undulograptus austrodentatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** Mitchell et al. (1997) *Episodes* 20:158–166.
- **Discussion:** A GSSP confirmed relatively early within the Ordovician. The *U. austrodentatus* FAD is a reference point for global graptolite stratigraphy.

#### Sandbian base (= base of the Upper Ordovician) — 458.4 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Fågelsång (Sularp Brook), Skåne, Sweden · graptolite *Nemagraptus gracilis* FAD
- **Ratification:** Bergström et al. (2000) *Episodes* 23:102–109.
- **Discussion:** The base of the Upper Ordovician. *N. gracilis* is a highly reliable marker owing to its global distribution. Also correlated with the GICE (Guttenberg) carbon isotope event.

#### Katian base — 453.0 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Black Knob Ridge, Oklahoma, USA · graptolite *Diplacanthograptus caudatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** Goldman et al. (2007) *Episodes* 30:258–270.
- **Discussion:** A graptolite-based GSSP in a Laurentian section. The Katian, one of the longest stages, spans the peak of biodiversity (late GOBE) and the precursors of later cooling.

#### Hirnantian base — 445.2 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Wangjiawan North, Yichang, China · graptolite *Normalograptus extraordinarius* FAD
- **Ratification:** Chen et al. (2006) *Episodes* 29:183–196.
- **Discussion:** The uppermost Ordovician. Corresponds to the expansion of the Gondwanan continental ice sheet and the **Late Ordovician mass extinction (LOME, ~85% of marine species lost)**. Near the boundary, the positive carbon isotope excursion **HICE**, coupled with the graptolite zonation (*N. extraordinarius* → *N. persculptus*), forms an axis of global correlation. The top is closed by the base of the Silurian (Rhuddanian).
- **Supporting papers:** *ratification* Chen et al. (2006) *Episodes* 29:183–196. *data* Gorjan, Kaiho, Fike & Chen (2012) "Carbon- and sulfur-isotope geochemistry of the Hirnantian (Late Ordovician) Wangjiawan (Riverside) section, South China," *Palaeogeography, Palaeoclimatology, Palaeoecology* 337–338:14–22, doi:10.1016/j.palaeo.2012.03.021 (HICE record in the section adjacent to the GSSP).

### Silurian System

> Many boundaries were confirmed in the 1980s (1982·1985 *Episodes*), and the two boundaries Sheinwoodian and Ludfordian remain "imprecise" owing to the absence of precise markers. The two lower Llandovery boundaries, Aeronian and Telychian, were **redefined** in January 2024 with new sections in the Czech Republic and Spain respectively, after the original Welsh standard sections proved imprecise and discontinuous. Most markers are graptolite FADs.

#### Rhuddanian base (= base of the Silurian) — 443.8 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Dob's Linn, Moffat, Scotland · graptolites *Akidograptus ascensus* / *Parakidograptus acuminatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1985; 8:101–103
- **Discussion:** The Ordovician/Silurian boundary and the recovery interval immediately following the end-Hirnantian mass extinction. Continuous deposition of black graptolitic shale gives it excellent long-distance correlation.

#### Aeronian base — 440.8 Ma · GSSP (redefined 2024)
- **Definition:** Hlásná Třebaň, Czech Republic (Barrandian, Želkovice Fm black shale) · graptolite *Demirastrites triangulatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** IUGS ratified January 2024. The original GSSP (Trefawr, central Wales) was abandoned and replaced owing to imprecision.
- **Discussion:** Studied over roughly a decade by Petr Štorch's team; the fourth Paleozoic GSSP in the Barrandian.
- **Supporting papers:** proposal Štorch, Manda, Tasáryová, Frýda, Chadimová & Melchin (2018) "A proposed new global stratotype for Aeronian Stage of the Silurian System: Hlásná Třebaň section, Czech Republic," *Lethaia* 51(3):357–388, doi:10.1111/let.12250. Ratified by IUGS EC 2024-01-09. A formal *Episodes* ratification paper is **not yet published** as of 2026 (confirmed only by the IUGS/ICS announcement).

#### Telychian base — 438.5 Ma · GSSP (redefined 2024)
- **Definition:** El Pintado reservoir, Cazalla de la Sierra, Seville, Spain · graptolite *Spirograptus guerichi* FAD
- **Ratification:** IUGS ratified January 2024. The original GSSP (1985, Llandovery, South Wales) was replaced owing to an unclear definition and the discovery of a sedimentary mélange.
- **Discussion:** A working group formed in 2014 reviewed candidates in Sweden, China, and Spain; in 2019 the Spanish section became the sole candidate → approved in 2023. El Pintado 1 is regarded as the only section that crosses the Aeronian/Telychian boundary in a continuously fossiliferous succession.
- **Supporting papers:** Gutiérrez-Marco, Loydell, Štorch & Frýda (2024) "El Pintado…, the seventh GSSP in Spain and the first in Andalusia (Telychian GSSP, Llandovery, Silurian)," *Geogaceta* 76:3–6, doi:10.55407/geogaceta104708.

#### Sheinwoodian base (= base of the Wenlock) — 433.4 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Hughley Brook, Shropshire, England · **IMPRECISE** — the interval between acritarch biozone 5 and the *Pterospathodus amorphognathoides* conodont zone
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1982; 5:21–23
- **Discussion:** Defined by an interval between microfossil zones rather than by a single-species FAD, so its precision is low.

#### Homerian base — 430.5 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Whitwell Coppice, Homer, England · graptolite *Cyrtograptus lundgreni* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1982; 5:21–23
- **Discussion:** Associated with the *lundgreni* event (immediately before the subsequent graptolite mass extinction), giving it high correlation value.

#### Gorstian base (= base of the Ludlow) — 427.4 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Pitch Coppice, Ludlow, England · graptolite *Saetograptus (Colonograptus) varians* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1982; 5:21–23
- **Discussion:** A classic section in the Ludlow standard area.

#### Ludfordian base — 425.6 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Sunnyhill, Ludlow, England · **IMPRECISE** — near the base of the *Saetograptus leintwardinensis* graptolite zone
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1982; 5:21–23
- **Discussion:** Defined as "near" the base of the graptolite zone rather than as a precise point, so its precision is low. A subject of subsequent chemostratigraphic study, such as the Lau carbon isotope excursion.

#### Přídolí base — 423.0 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Požáry, Prague, Czech Republic (Barrandian) · graptolite *Monograptus parultimus* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1985; 8:101–103
- **Discussion:** The uppermost series of the Silurian, using a regional name directly without a separate rank designation. It takes advantage of the continuous Barrandian section.

### Devonian System

> All 7 boundaries are ratified. The Lochkovian (= base of the Devonian) is **one of the first-generation GSSPs**, established at Klonk in 1972. The lower part is based on conodont lineages, and the middle and upper parts on *Polygnathus*·*Ancyrodella*·*Palmatolepis* conodont FADs. **The Emsian GSSP is under revision.** The Frasnian/Famennian boundary coincides with the Kellwasser mass extinction and has recently been precisely recalibrated with U-Pb and astrochronology.

#### Lochkovian base (= base of the Devonian = Silurian/Devonian boundary) — 419.2 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Klonk, Czech Republic (Barrandian) · graptolite *Monograptus uniformis* FAD
- **Ratification:** confirmed 1972 (one of the earliest GSSPs); age value from GTS2020
- **Discussion:** A symbolic section from the period when the GSSP concept was being established. Carbon-cycle and U-Pb studies crossing the boundary have been carried out in recent years.

#### Pragian base — 410.8 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Velká Chuchle, Prague, Czech Republic · conodont *Eognathodus sulcatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1989; 12:109–113
- **Discussion:** Based on the Barrandian Praha Limestone. Research on lower Devonian conodont lineages is active, entangled with the Pragian/Emsian boundary debate.

#### Emsian base — 407.6 Ma · GSSP (revision in progress)
- **Definition:** Zinzil'ban Gorge, Uzbekistan (Kitab Reserve) · conodont *Polygnathus kitabicus* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1997; 20:235–240
- **Discussion:** The SDS decided in 2008 to revise the current GSSP. This is because the boundary defined by *P. kitabicus* correlates with the lower part of the classic German area and the Bohemian Praha Limestone, placing it markedly lower than the traditional Emsian. A proposal to raise the base to near the appearance of *Polygnathus excavatus* was submitted, and international expeditions were carried out in the Kitab Reserve in 2008 and 2015. **As of 2018 at the proposal stage; ratification evidence for 2024–2025 unverified.**
- **Supporting papers:** revision rationale Carls, Slavík & Valenzuela-Ríos (2008) "Comments on the GSSP for the basal Emsian stage boundary: the need for its redefinition," *Bulletin of Geosciences* 83(4):383–390, doi:10.3140/bull.geosci.2008.04.383.

#### Eifelian base (= base of the Middle Devonian) — 393.3 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Wetteldorf Richtschnitt, Germany (Eifel) · conodont *Polygnathus costatus partitus* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1985; 8:104–109
- **Discussion:** The Lower/Middle Devonian boundary. High-precision U-Pb ages from the nearby Hercules K-bentonite (e.g. Hercules I 394.290 ± 0.097 Ma) are the key anchor for calibrating this interval.
- **Supporting papers:** Harrigan, Schmitz, Over, Trayler & Davydov (2022) "Recalibrating the Devonian time scale: A new method for integrating radioisotopic and astrochronologic ages in a Bayesian framework," *GSA Bulletin* 134(7–8):1931–1948, doi:10.1130/B36128.1.

#### Givetian base — 387.7 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Jebel Mech Irdane, Tafilalt, Morocco · conodont *Polygnathus hemiansatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1995; 18:107–115
- **Discussion:** A continuous pelagic limestone section in the Anti-Atlas. Supported by research refining Moroccan conodont stratigraphy.

#### Frasnian base (= base of the Upper Devonian) — 382.7 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Col du Puech de la Suque, Montagne Noire, France · conodont *Ancyrodella rotundiloba* FAD
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1987; 10:97–101
- **Discussion:** A pelagic section in the Montagne Noire, France. One of the reference intervals for upper Devonian conodont biostratigraphy.

#### Famennian base — 372.2 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Coumiac Quarry, Montagne Noire, France · conodont *Palmatolepis triangularis* FAD/abundance
- **Ratification:** *Episodes* 1993; 16:433–441
- **Discussion:** Coincides with the **Kellwasser (Late Devonian) mass extinction**, one of the "Big Five." It is characterized by two oceanic anoxic events in the uppermost Frasnian, a positive δ¹³C excursion, and reef-ecosystem collapse. Recently recalibrated to 372–371.8 Ma by integrating K-bentonite U-Pb ID-TIMS with astrochronology (GTS2020 adopts 372.2 Ma).
- **Supporting papers:** Percival et al. (2018) "Precisely dating the Frasnian–Famennian boundary…"; De Vleeschouwer et al. (2020) "Anchoring the Late Devonian mass extinction in absolute time…" *Scientific Reports* 10.

### Carboniferous System

> Divided into two subsystems — the lower **Mississippian** (Tournaisian·Visean·Serpukhovian) and the upper **Pennsylvanian** (Bashkirian·Moscovian·Kasimovian·Gzhelian). Of its 7 stages, only 3 are defined — Tournaisian, Visean, and Bashkirian — while the remaining 4 are undefined. Even the existing Tournaisian (= base of the Carboniferous) GSSP is under discussion for redefinition owing to marker problems, making this one of the least stable systems in the Phanerozoic in terms of definition status.

#### base of Tournaisian (base of Mississippian = Devonian/Carboniferous boundary = base of the Carboniferous) — 358.9 Ma · GSSP (redefinition under discussion)
- **Definition:** La Serre (E′ trench), Montagne Noire, France · conodont *Siphonodella sulcata* FAD
- **Ratification:** Paproth, Feist & Flajs (1991) *Episodes* 14:331–336
- **Discussion:** Currently **assessed as a defective GSSP and targeted for redefinition**. The index species *S. sulcata* is difficult to distinguish from its ancestral form *S. praesulcata*, and problems with reworking and correlation precision of the FAD itself have been confirmed since 2006. The Devonian–Carboniferous Boundary Working Group (established 2008) is reviewing new markers and a defining stratigraphy, but as of 2026 no replacement GSSP has been **proposed or ratified**. Candidates such as Borkewehr, Germany are under discussion.
- **Supporting papers:** redefinition review Aretz & Corradini (2021) "Global review of the Devonian-Carboniferous Boundary: an introduction," *Palaeobiodiversity and Palaeoenvironments* 101(2):285–293, doi:10.1007/s12549-021-00499-8.

#### base of Visean (Mississippian) — 346.7 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Pengchong, Guangxi, China · foraminifer *Eoparastaffella simplex* FAD (*E. rotunda → E. simplex* lineage)
- **Ratification:** Devuyst, Hance, Hou et al. (2003) *Episodes* 26:105–115
- **Discussion:** The only Mississippian boundary defined by a microfossil (foraminifer). Combined conodont–foraminifer correlation is relatively stable.

#### base of Serpukhovian (uppermost Mississippian) — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont *Lochriea ziegleri* FAD (*L. nodosa → L. ziegleri* lineage)
- **Ratification:** undefined (candidates: Verkhnyaya Kardailovka, Russia / Naqing (Nashui), China)
- **Discussion:** *L. ziegleri* is widely used in practical correlation but has not been formally ratified. The two candidates — the southern Urals of Russia and southern China — are in competition.

#### base of Bashkirian (base of Pennsylvanian) — 323.2 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Arrow Canyon, Nevada, USA · conodont *Declinognathodus noduliferus* FAD
- **Ratification:** Lane, Brenckle, Baesemann & Richards (1999) *Episodes* 22:272–283
- **Discussion:** The Mississippian/Pennsylvanian subsystem boundary. A carbonate-platform section in the western USA.

#### base of Moscovian (Pennsylvanian) — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont *Diplognathodus ellesmerensis* or *Declinognathodus donetzianus* FAD (undefined)
- **Discussion:** The choice of marker species itself is unresolved. Prospects are uncertain owing to debate over the phylogeny and appearance timing of the candidate species.

#### base of Kasimovian (Pennsylvanian) — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** no agreed marker (fusulinid foraminifera and conodont candidates under review)
- **Ratification:** undefined (candidates: southern Urals, southwestern USA, Nashui, China)
- **Discussion:** Of the 4 undefined boundaries, marker agreement lags furthest here.

#### base of Gzhelian (uppermost Pennsylvanian) — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont *Idiognathodus simulator* (s.s.) FAD
- **Discussion:** Marker agreement is relatively advanced, but a formal proposal and ratification have not yet been reached.

### Permian System

> Divided into three series — the lower **Cisuralian** (Asselian·Sakmarian·Artinskian·Kungurian), the middle **Guadalupian** (Roadian·Wordian·Capitanian), and the upper **Lopingian** (Wuchiapingian·Changhsingian). The recent successive ratifications of Sakmarian (2020), Capitanian (2022), Artinskian (2023), and the Wuchiapingian redefinition (2024) leave **only Kungurian undefined**. It is a well-time-calibrated system in which CA-ID-TIMS U-Pb ages and conodont biostratigraphy are tightly integrated.

#### base of Asselian (base of Cisuralian = Carboniferous/Permian boundary = base of the Permian) — 298.9 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Aidaralash Creek, Kazakhstan · conodont *Streptognathodus isolatus* FAD
- **Ratification:** Davydov, Glenister, Spinosa et al. (1998) *Episodes* 21:11–18
- **Discussion:** The Carboniferous/Permian boundary. A GTS anchor precisely calibrated by the Ramezani et al. (2007) CA-ID-TIMS age (298.90 ± 0.15 Ma).
- **Supporting papers:** *age model* Ramezani, Schmitz, Davydov, Bowring, Snyder & Northrup (2007) "High-precision U–Pb zircon age constraints on the Carboniferous–Permian boundary in the southern Urals stratotype," *Earth and Planetary Science Letters* 256(3–4):244–257, doi:10.1016/j.epsl.2007.01.032.

#### base of Sakmarian (Cisuralian) — 293.52 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Usolka, southern Urals, Russia · conodont *Mesogondolella monstra* FAD (used together with the *Sweetognathus* lineage)
- **Ratification:** Chernykh, Chuvashov, Shen et al. (2020) *Episodes* 43(4):961–979
- **Discussion:** Usolka contains numerous radiometric volcanic-ash beds, directly linking U-Pb ages with conodont biostratigraphy.
- **Supporting papers:** *age model* Schmitz & Davydov (2012) "Quantitative radiometric and biostratigraphic calibration of the Pennsylvanian–Early Permian (Cisuralian) time scale…," *GSA Bulletin* 124(3–4):549–577, doi:10.1130/B30385.1.

#### base of Artinskian (Cisuralian) — 290.1 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Dalny Tulkas, southern Urals, Russia · conodont *Sweetognathus asymmetricus* FAD (formerly *S. whitei*, redefined)
- **Ratification:** Chuvashov, Chernykh, Schmitz et al. (2023) *Episodes* 46(4):623–651
- **Discussion:** The most recently defined Cisuralian boundary. Note that the marker species name was redefined from *S. whitei* → *S. asymmetricus*.

#### base of Kungurian (uppermost Cisuralian) — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont *Neostreptognathodus pnevi* FAD
- **Ratification:** undefined (candidate: Mechetlino, southern Urals, Russia)
- **Discussion:** The only undefined boundary in the Permian. Given the recent trend of Cisuralian ratifications, future ratification is likely (timing **unverified**).

#### base of Roadian (base of Guadalupian) — 273.01 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Stratotype Canyon, Guadalupe Mts, Texas, USA · conodont *Jinogondolella nankingensis* FAD
- **Ratification:** Glenister, Wardlaw, Lambert, Spinosa, Bowring, Erwin, Menning & Wilde (1999) "Proposal of Guadalupian and component Roadian, Wordian and Capitanian Stages as international standards for the Middle Permian Series," *Permophiles* 34:3–11. (Guadalupian GSSP proposal; IUGS ratified 2001.)
- **Discussion:** All three Guadalupian boundaries are defined by the *Jinogondolella* lineage, making intra-series correlation consistent. The Texas standard, however, is limited in long-range correlation with the Tethys.

#### base of Wordian (Guadalupian) — 266.9 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Guadalupe Pass, Texas, USA · conodont *Jinogondolella aserrata* FAD
- **Ratification:** Glenister, Wardlaw, Lambert et al. (1999) *Permophiles* 34:3–11 (Guadalupian proposal; IUGS ratified 2001).
- **Discussion:** Research indicating that the early eruptive pulses of the Emeishan LIP correspond to the lowermost Wordian has prompted discussion of linkage with middle Permian environmental change.

#### base of Capitanian (uppermost Guadalupian) — 264.28 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Nipple Hill, Guadalupe Mts, Texas, USA · conodont *Jinogondolella postserrata* FAD
- **Ratification:** Wardlaw, Lambert, Shen et al. (2022) *Episodes* 45(3):309–331
- **Discussion:** Associated with the **Capitanian (middle Permian) mass extinction**, a crisis temporally coincident with pulsed eruptions of the **Emeishan LIP** (interpreted as volcanism → marine anoxia/warming → extinction trigger).
- **Supporting papers:** *extinction·LIP* Wei, Zhang & Qiu (2026) "Pulsed volcanism of the Emeishan large igneous province caused the Guadalupian (Middle Permian) mass extinction," *GSA Bulletin* 138(7–8):3233–3246, doi:10.1130/B38771.1. *extinction synthesis* Bond, Hilton, Wignall, Ali, Stevens, Sun & Lai (2010) "The Middle Permian (Capitanian) mass extinction on land and in the oceans," *Earth-Science Reviews* 102(3–4):100–116, doi:10.1016/j.earscirev.2010.07.004.

#### base of Wuchiapingian (base of Lopingian) — 259.51 Ma · GSSP (redefined 2024)
- **Definition:** Penglaitan, Guangxi, China · conodont *Clarkina postbitteri postbitteri* FAD
- **Ratification:** original definition Jin, Shen, Henderson et al. (2006 *Episodes* 29(2):253–262 series); **redefined** Shen, Yuan, Henderson et al. (2024) *Episodes* 47(1):147–177
- **Discussion:** The Guadalupian/Lopingian boundary. The original GSSP site was **submerged by flooding in 2020**, so it was redefined in 2024 to include a newly excavated nearby GSSP plus the Fengshan Supplementary Auxiliary Boundary Stratotype (SABS). U-Pb dating of a tuff of Emeishan basalt origin, **259.51 ± 0.21 Ma**, provides the age basis. A representative case of GSSP redefinition (in response to submergence).

#### base of Changhsingian (uppermost Lopingian) — 254.14 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Meishan (D), Zhejiang, China · conodont *Clarkina wangi* FAD (*C. longicuspidata → C. wangi* lineage)
- **Ratification:** Jin, Wang, Henderson et al. (2006) *Episodes* 29(3):175–182
- **Discussion:** The uppermost stage of the Permian. Its upper limit (= Permian/Triassic boundary ~251.9 Ma) coincides with the greatest **end-Permian mass extinction** in history and the Siberian Traps LIP. Meishan contains numerous CA-ID-TIMS age beds, constraining the absolute age and duration of the crisis with extreme precision.
- **Supporting papers:** *age model* Burgess, Bowring & Shen (2014) *PNAS* — Meishan volcanic-ash CA-ID-TIMS. *synthesis* Ramezani & Bowring (2018) in *The Permian Timescale*, Geol. Soc. London SP450 — Permian upper/lower limits 298.92 ± 0.19 / 251.90 ± 0.10 Ma.

### Triassic System

> Of the 7 stages, only **3** are defined (Induan, Ladinian, Carnian), while the other 4 (Olenekian, Anisian, Norian, Rhaetian) are undefined. The base of the lower Induan is the Permian/Triassic boundary and coincides with the largest mass extinction in history (end-Permian), while the upper Triassic/Jurassic boundary likewise overlaps with the end-Triassic mass extinction.

#### base of the Induan (= base of the Triassic = Permian/Triassic boundary) — 251.902 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Meishan, Zhejiang, China · conodont *Hindeodus parvus* FAD
- **Ratification:** Yin et al. (2001) *Episodes* 24:102–114
- **Discussion:** Coincides with the largest mass extinction of the Phanerozoic. High-precision U-Pb dating has shown that the extinction interval spans 251.941 ± 0.037 to 251.880 ± 0.031 Ma, only about 60 ± 48 kyr, and a causal link to the Siberian Traps LIP is strongly supported.
- **Supporting papers:** *age model* Burgess, Bowring & Shen (2014) *PNAS* 111:3316–3321. *extinction* Shen et al. (2011) "Calibrating the End-Permian Mass Extinction," *Science* 334(6061):1367–1372, doi:10.1126/science.1213454.

#### base of the Olenekian — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont *Neospathodus (Novispathodus) waageni* FAD
- **Ratification:** undefined (candidates: Chaohu, China / Guryul Ravine·Spiti, India)
- **Discussion:** Taxonomic revision of the marker species group is the central issue, and convergence on a single GSSP has not yet been reached.

#### base of the Anisian (= base of the Middle Triassic) — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont *Chiosella timorensis* FAD
- **Ratification:** undefined (candidates: Deşli Caira, Romania / Kçira, Albania)
- **Discussion:** Deşli Caira is the leading candidate, with new conodont data still being reported in 2023–2025, but it remains unratified.

#### base of the Ladinian — 242 Ma (or 241.5) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Bagolino, Lombardy, Italy · ammonite *Eoprotrachyceras curionii* FAD
- **Ratification:** Brack et al. (2005) *Episodes* 28:233–244
- **Discussion:** The U-Pb age of the Bagolino volcanic ash is an anchor of the Middle Triassic time scale. It settled the classic Alpine Triassic stratigraphic debate (the lower limit of the Ladinian).

#### base of the Carnian (= base of the Upper Triassic) — ~237 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Prati di Stuores/Stuores Wiesen, Dolomites, Italy · ammonoid *Daxatina canadensis* FAD
- **Ratification:** Mietto et al. (2012) *Episodes* 35:414–430
- **Discussion:** The early Carnian is associated with a humidification event (the Carnian Pluvial Episode).

#### base of the Norian — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont/ammonoid (*Stikinoceras kerri* zone series)
- **Ratification:** undefined. A 2021 working group vote (about 60% in favor) selected **Pizzo Mondello (Italy)** as the preferred candidate, and review/ratification procedures were put forward, but it remains unratified as of 2025–2026 (the other candidate being Black Bear Ridge, Canada).
- **Discussion:** As one of the longest ages in the Phanerozoic, internal subdivision and the establishment of radiometric ages remain challenges.

#### base of the Rhaetian — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker conodont *Misikella posthernsteini* FAD
- **Ratification:** undefined (candidates: Steinbergkogel, Austria / Pignola-Abriola, Italy)
- **Discussion:** Marker and magnetostratigraphic correlation continue to be refined.

### Jurassic System

> Of the 11 ages, **8** are defined, while 3 (Callovian, Oxfordian, Tithonian) are undefined. The Jurassic/Cretaceous boundary is the only system boundary in the Phanerozoic that lacks a GSSP. The base of the lower Hettangian is the Triassic/Jurassic boundary and is associated with the end-Triassic mass extinction and CAMP volcanism.

#### base of the Hettangian (= base of the Jurassic = Triassic/Jurassic boundary) — 201.3 Ma (or 201.4) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Kuhjoch, Karwendel, Austria · ammonite *Psiloceras spelae tirolicum* FO
- **Ratification:** Hillebrandt et al. (2013) *Episodes* 36:162–198
- **Discussion:** Immediately after the end-Triassic mass extinction. The synchronicity between the eruption of CAMP (Central Atlantic Magmatic Province) basalts and the extinction is supported by U-Pb dating. Newark–Hartford basin astrochronology is the verification axis for this interval.
- **Supporting papers:** *age model/CAMP* Blackburn et al. (2013) "Zircon U-Pb Geochronology Links the End-Triassic Extinction with the CAMP," *Science* 340:941–945. *astrochronology* Kent, Olsen & Muttoni (2017) "Astrochronostratigraphic polarity time scale (APTS) for the Late Triassic and Early Jurassic…," *Earth-Science Reviews* 166:153–180, doi:10.1016/j.earscirev.2016.12.014 (Newark–Hartford APTS).

#### base of the Sinemurian — 199.3 Ma (or 199.5) · GSSP
- **Definition:** East Quantoxhead, Somerset, England · ammonite *Vermiceras quantoxense* FAD
- **Ratification:** Bloos & Page (2002) *Episodes* 25:22–28
- **Discussion:** One of the anchor sections of the standard Lower Jurassic ammonite biostratigraphy.

#### base of the Pliensbachian — 190.8 Ma (or 192.9) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Robin Hood's Bay, Yorkshire, England · ammonite *Bifericeras donovani* / *Apoderoceras* datum
- **Ratification:** Meister et al. (2006) *Episodes* 29:93–106
- **Discussion:** An interval with a large difference in numerical age between GTS2020 and earlier editions (≈190.8 vs 192.9 Ma), so the value is fluid depending on calibration.

#### base of the Toarcian — 182.7 Ma (or 184.2) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Peniche, Portugal · ammonite *Dactylioceras (Eodactylites) simplex* FO
- **Ratification:** Rocha et al. (2016) *Episodes* 39(3):460–481
- **Discussion:** The early Toarcian is associated with an oceanic anoxic event (T-OAE), a large-scale carbon isotope excursion, and Karoo–Ferrar volcanism.
- **Supporting papers:** *T-OAE* Hesselbo et al. (2000) "Massive dissociation of gas hydrate during a Jurassic oceanic anoxic event," *Nature* 406:392–395.

#### base of the Aalenian (= base of the Middle Jurassic) — 174.1 Ma (or 174.7) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Fuentelsaz, Spain · ammonite *Leioceras opalinum* FAD
- **Ratification:** Cresta et al. (2001) *Episodes* 24:166–175

#### base of the Bajocian — 170.3 Ma (or 170.9) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Cabo Mondego, Portugal · ammonite *Hyperlioceras mundum* FAD
- **Ratification:** Pavia & Enay (1997) *Episodes* 20:16–22

#### base of the Bathonian — 168.3 Ma (or 168.2) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Ravin du Bès, Bas-Auran, Alpes-de-Haute-Provence, France · ammonite *Gonolkites convergens* FAD
- **Ratification:** Fernández-López et al. (2009) *Episodes* 32:222–248

#### base of the Callovian — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker appearance of the ammonite genus *Kepplerites*
- **Discussion:** The uppermost age of the Middle Jurassic, and the only remaining undefined interval given that the Aalenian–Bathonian are all ratified.

#### base of the Oxfordian (= base of the Upper Jurassic) — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker ammonite *Cardioceras redcliffense* / *Brightia thuouxensis* horizon
- **Ratification:** undefined. As of the 2024 IUGS/ICS report it is still unratified, with candidates narrowed to two sites, **Thuoux (SE France)** and **Redcliff Point, Weymouth (England)**.
- **Discussion:** The Callovian/Oxfordian boundary is delayed by difficulties in correlating magnetostratigraphy and ammonite provinces (Subboreal vs Submediterranean).

#### base of the Kimmeridgian — 154.8 Ma (or 157.3) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Flodigarry, Isle of Skye, Scotland · base of the Subboreal ammonite Baylei Zone / *Pictonia baylei*
- **Ratification:** ratified around 2021; formally published in *Episodes* (2023) 46(2):281–307
- **Discussion:** A relatively recent GSSP defined by an integrated stratigraphy linking the Boreal, Subboreal, and Submediterranean ammonite provinces.

#### base of the Tithonian — undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate marker base of magnetostratigraphic Chron **M22An** / ammonite (with the appearance of the genus *Gravesia* as a working definition, etc.)
- **Discussion:** The uppermost age of the Jurassic. Above it, the Jurassic/Cretaceous boundary is the only interval in the Phanerozoic lacking a system boundary GSSP. Magnetostratigraphic correlation is the central axis of the definition.

### Cretaceous System

> The most fluid of all systems, with many of its lower boundaries newly defined in recent years. Following Hauterivian (2021), Coniacian (2022), and Campanian (2022), and Barremian (2024), the **Valanginian was ratified in December 2024** (Episodes 2025). Even so, the **base of the Berriasian (= base of the Cretaceous = Jurassic/Cretaceous boundary) and the base of the Aptian remain undefined and disputed**, so the lower limit of the Cretaceous itself is not yet fixed as a standard point. Markers split between ammonites, calpionellids, and magnetostratigraphy in the lower part, and planktonic foraminifera, inoceramids, and magnetic reversals in the upper part. *(Lower: Berriasian→Albian / Upper: Cenomanian→Maastrichtian)*

#### base of the Berriasian (= base of the Cretaceous = Jurassic/Cretaceous boundary) — ~143.1 Ma (or 145.0) · undefined/disputed
- **Definition:** Candidate section Tré Maroua (SE France) — candidate marker base of the *Calpionella alpina* Subzone (calpionellid Zone B) / magnetic Chron M19n. Alternatives: Vaca Muerta (Argentina), ammonite *Berriasella jacobi* FAD.
- **Ratification:** Undefined and disputed. The 2019 Tré Maroua proposal (base of the *C. alpina* Subzone) was **rejected by the International Subcommission on Cretaceous Stratigraphy (ISCS) in October 2020**, and a new working group is being formed. Proposal by Wimbledon et al. (2020) "The proposal of a GSSP for the Berriasian Stage (Cretaceous System): Part 1," *Volumina Jurassica* XVIII(1):53–106, doi:10.7306/vj.18.5 (Part 2: doi:10.7306/vj.18.6).
- **Discussion:** One of the few Phanerozoic system boundaries without a GSSP. The core difficulty is **global correlatability**: Tethyan calpionellids are poorly represented at high latitudes, and there are concerns about stratigraphic mismatch and diachroneity among the candidate markers (base of the calpionellid subzone, M19n, ammonite FAD). Age values also coexist between GTS2020 ~145.0 Ma and a recalibrated ~143.1 Ma.
- **Supporting papers:** Magnetic M-sequence Malinverno, Hildebrandt, Tominaga & Channell (2012) "M-sequence geomagnetic polarity time scale (MHTC12)…," *J. Geophys. Res. Solid Earth* 117(B6):B06104, doi:10.1029/2012JB009260. J/K boundary synthesis Wimbledon et al. (2020) *Volumina Jurassica* XVIII(1):53–106.

#### base of the Valanginian — ~137.05 Ma (or 139.8) · GSSP (ratified 2024)
- **Definition:** Vergol section, Montbrun-les-Bains, Drôme, France (base 19.23 m, base of VGL-B136). Auxiliary section Cañada Luenga (Cehegín, SE Spain). Marker ammonite *Thurmanniceras pertransiens* FO — recently reassigned to *Hoedemaekeria pertransiens*. The calpionellid *Calpionellites darderi* FO serves as a near-synchronous auxiliary marker.
- **Ratification:** Reboulet et al. (2025) *Episodes* 48(4):479–563. Approved by the IUGS Executive Committee in **December 2024**, published 2025.
- **Discussion:** The inconsistent status across sources was resolved by the 2024 ratification. The 1995 Brussels meeting recommended the base of the *C. darderi* Zone, but the final proposal adopted the ammonite FO as the primary marker (the two events being effectively simultaneous). An asymmetric situation in which the upper boundary of the Berriasian is defined while its lower boundary (= base of the Berriasian) remains undetermined.
- **Supporting papers:** Candidate section details Kenjo, Reboulet, Mattioli & Ma'louleh (2021) "The Berriasian–Valanginian boundary… Vergol section (Montbrun-les-Bains, SE France), candidate for the Valanginian GSSP," *Cretaceous Research* 121:104738, doi:10.1016/j.cretres.2020.104738. Astrochronology Martinez, Deconinck, Pellenard, Reboulet & Riquier (2014) "Astrochronology of the Valanginian Stage from GSSP Candidates and Hypostratotype," in *STRATI 2013*, Springer Geology, pp. 209–212, doi:10.1007/978-3-319-04364-7_42.

#### base of the Hauterivian — 132.6 Ma (or 132.9) · GSSP
- **Definition:** La Charce, Drôme, France · ammonite genus *Acanthodiscus* (particularly *A. radiatus*) FO
- **Ratification:** Mutterlose et al. (2021) *Episodes* 44(2):129–150
- **Discussion:** A classic Lower Cretaceous GSSP demonstrating that the Vocontian Basin is the standard arena for Tethyan ammonite and nannofossil stratigraphy.

#### base of the Barremian — 125.77 Ma · GSSP (ratified 2024)
- **Definition:** Río Argos, Caravaca, Murcia, Spain · ammonite *Taveraidiscus hugii* (formerly *Spitidiscus hugii*) FAD
- **Ratification:** Company, Aguado, Baudin, Coccioni et al. (2024) "The Global Boundary Stratotype Section and Point (GSSP) of the Barremian Stage at Río Argos (Caravaca, SE Spain)," *Episodes* 47(2):335–379, doi:10.18814/epiiugs/2023/02330.
- **Discussion:** Reaffirms that most Lower Cretaceous boundary markers rely on ammonite FADs. The generic reassignment (*Spitidiscus*→*Taveraidiscus*) shows that the taxonomy is being revised.

#### base of the Aptian — ~121.4 Ma (or 125.0) · undefined
- **Definition:** Candidate section Gorgo a Cerbara (Umbria-Marche, Italy). Candidate marker base of magnetic Chron **M0r**; alternative the initial negative δ¹³C peak excursion of **OAE1a (Selli Level)**.
- **Ratification:** Undefined. As of 2025, in candidate status (under ISCS review).
- **Discussion:** The proposal to use the base of the M0r magnetic reversal was dominant, but reinterpretive critiques (Newsletters on Stratigraphy 2017, 2019) have been raised regarding the use of M0r as a datum at Gorgo a Cerbara. The working group is leaning toward recommending the maximum of the initial δ¹³C excursion of OAE1a (Selli) as the GSSP — the shift from a magnetostratigraphic to a chemostratigraphic marker is the crux of the debate. The lower-limit age also coexists between ~121 and ~125 Ma.
- **Supporting papers:** *OAE1a* Menegatti, Weissert, Brown, Tyson, Farrimond, Strasser & Caron (1998) "High-resolution δ¹³C stratigraphy through the Early Aptian 'Livello Selli' of the Alpine Tethys," *Paleoceanography* 13(5):530–545, doi:10.1029/98PA01793.

#### base of the Albian — 113.0 Ma (or 113.2) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Col de Pré-Guittard, Arnayon, Drôme, France · planktonic foraminifer *Microhedbergella renilaevis* FO
- **Ratification:** Kennedy et al. (2017) *Episodes* 40(3):177–188
- **Discussion:** The point at the top of the Lower Cretaceous where the marker shifts from ammonites to **planktonic foraminifera**, serving as a bridge into Upper Cretaceous foraminiferal stratigraphy.

#### base of the Cenomanian (= base of the Upper Cretaceous) — 100.5 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Mont Risou, Hautes-Alpes, France · planktonic foraminifer *Rotalipora globotruncanoides* FAD
- **Ratification:** Kennedy et al. (2004) *Episodes* 27:21–32
- **Discussion:** The Lower/Upper Cretaceous (and Series) boundary. Adopts a planktonic foraminifer, part of the standard Upper Cretaceous marker lineage.

#### base of the Turonian — 93.9 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Pueblo (Rock Canyon), Colorado, USA · ammonite *Watinoceras devonense* FAD
- **Ratification:** Kennedy et al. (2005) *Episodes* 28:93–104
- **Discussion:** The boundary lies just above **OAE2 (the Cenomanian–Turonian boundary event, Bonarelli Level)**, giving excellent correlation via the global positive carbon-isotope excursion. A rare case of adopting a section outside Europe (North America) as a GSSP.
- **Supporting papers:** *OAE2 age model* Sageman, Meyers & Arthur (2006) "Orbital time scale and new C-isotope record for Cenomanian–Turonian boundary stratotype," *Geology* 34(2):125–128, doi:10.1130/G22074.1.

#### base of the Coniacian — 89.8 Ma · GSSP (ratified 2022)
- **Definition:** Salzgitter-Salder, Germany · inoceramid *Cremnoceramus deformis erectus* (=*C. rotundatus*) FO
- **Ratification:** Walaszczyk, Čech, Crampton, Dubicka et al. (2022) "The Global Boundary Stratotype Section and Point (GSSP) for the base of the Coniacian Stage (Salzgitter-Salder, Germany) and its auxiliary sections," *Episodes* 45(2):181–220, doi:10.18814/epiiugs/2021/021022.
- **Discussion:** The Turonian/Coniacian boundary is difficult to correlate by foraminifera and ammonites, so a Boreal-reliable **inoceramid** was adopted.

#### base of the Santonian — 86.3 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Olazagutia (Cantera de Margas), Spain · inoceramid *Platyceramus undulatoplicatus* FO
- **Ratification:** Lamolda et al. (2014) *Episodes* 37:2–13
- **Discussion:** The *P. undulatoplicatus* FO is an event that is well tracked globally.

#### base of the Campanian — ~83.6 Ma (range 82.7–84.5) · GSSP (ratified ~2022)
- **Definition:** Bottaccione, Gubbio, Italy · **magnetic reversal C34n→C33r** (base of C33r; just after the end of the Cretaceous Normal Superchron)
- **Ratification:** Gale, Batenburg, Coccioni, Dubicka et al. (2023) "The Global Boundary Stratotype Section and Point (GSSP) of the Campanian Stage at Bottaccione (Gubbio, Italy) and its Auxiliary Sections," *Episodes* 46(3):451–490, doi:10.18814/epiiugs/2022/022048.
- **Discussion:** The only boundary in the Upper Cretaceous to use **magnetostratigraphy as its primary marker**. While reliable biostratigraphic events are hard to find, the end of the Cretaceous Normal Superchron is distinct and globally correlatable. The broad age uncertainty (82.7–84.5 Ma) reflects the calibration difficulty of this interval.

#### base of the Maastrichtian — 72.1 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Tercis les Bains, Landes, France · **the mean position of 12 biostratigraphic criteria** (represented notably by the ammonite *Pachydiscus neubergicus* interval)
- **Ratification:** Odin & Lamaurelle (2001) *Episodes* 24:229–238
- **Discussion:** An unusual approach that takes the boundary not as a single-species FAD but as **the mean horizon of several biostratigraphic events** (a compromise in a situation where no single marker correlates perfectly on a global scale). The upper boundary is the **K-Pg boundary**, whose GSSP is separately defined at El Kef (Tunisia). Closely tied to studies of the K-Pg iridium anomaly and impact stratigraphy.
- **Supporting papers:** *K-Pg age model* Renne et al. (2013) *Science* 339:684–687 — ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar K-Pg / Chicxulub recalibration.

### Paleogene System

> The first system of the Cenozoic. Its base, the K-Pg boundary, is the most dramatic of all boundaries — the Chicxulub asteroid impact, the iridium anomaly, and a mass extinction all coincide on a single bedding plane. Internally it contains the PETM, a natural experiment in global warming, making it the stage where carbon-isotope stratigraphy and astronomical tuning are first introduced in earnest. Three series: Paleocene, Eocene, Oligocene.

#### Danian base (= base of Paleogene and Paleocene = K-Pg boundary) — 66.0 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** El Kef, Tunisia · base of the iridium anomaly at the K-Pg impact/extinction horizon
- **Ratification:** Molina et al. (2006) *Episodes* 29:263–278 (concept first proposed 1991, El Kef)
- **Discussion:** The iridium-enriched layer from the Chicxulub impactor serves as the marker — a rare case in which impact, extinction, and boundary physically coincide. U-Pb and ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar converge on 66.0 Ma, anchoring the Cenozoic astronomical time scale.
- **Supporting papers:** *age model* Renne et al. (2013) *Science* 339:684–687 (Chicxulub sanidine ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar, 66.043 ± 0.043 Ma).

#### Selandian base — 61.6 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Zumaia, Basque Country, Spain · calcareous microfossils (second radiation of *Fasciculithus*) / carbon-isotope shift
- **Ratification:** Schmitz et al. (2011) *Episodes* 34:220–243
- **Discussion:** The deep-marine hemipelagic section at Zumaia preserves microfossil and magnetostratigraphy simultaneously, allowing the two boundaries — Selandian and Thanetian — to be defined continuously.

#### Thanetian base — 59.2 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Zumaia, Spain · base of magnetic chron C26n
- **Ratification:** Schmitz et al. (2011) *Episodes* 34:220–243
- **Discussion:** Defined within the same section using a magnetic reversal as the primary marker, supported by cyclostratigraphy for astronomical tuning.

#### Ypresian base (= base of Eocene = P/E boundary) — 56.0 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Dababiya, Luxor, Egypt · base of the carbon-isotope excursion (CIE) accompanying the PETM
- **Ratification:** Aubry et al. (2007) *Episodes* 30:271–286
- **Discussion:** A representative case in which the boundary is defined by a sharp δ¹³C drop (CIE) — the geochemical signal itself is the boundary. The PETM is intensively studied as a paleoclimatic analog of rapid warming and ocean acidification.
- **Supporting papers:** *astronomical age* Zeebe & Lourens (2019) *Science* 365:926–929 (ZB18a, 56.01 ± 0.05 Ma); integrated Cenozoic calibration Westerhold et al. (2020) *Science* 369:1383–1387 (CENOGRID).

#### Lutetian base (= base of middle Eocene) — 47.8 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Gorrondatxe, Basque Country, Spain · calcareous microfossil *Blackites inflatus* LO (secondarily *Hantkenina* FAD)
- **Ratification:** Molina et al. (2011) *Episodes* 34:86–108
- **Discussion:** A nannofossil datum anchored in an astronomically tuned turbidite section, providing a precise orbital-chronology framework for the middle Eocene.

#### Bartonian base — ~41.0 Ma · undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate Contessa Highway, Gubbio, Italy · near the LAD of *Reticulofenestra reticulata* (astronomically in the upper part of C19n ≈ 41.2 Ma)
- **Ratification:** undefined. As of 2025, the only undefined boundary in the Paleogene. Contessa Highway has been noted as less than ideal owing to faulting, induration, and outcrop cover.
- **Discussion:** Despite decades of candidate studies, still unratified. The conventional numerical age (~41.0 Ma) is a provisional calibration value.
- **Supporting papers:** Jovane, Sprovieri, Coccioni, Florindo, Marsili & Laskar (2010) "Astronomical calibration of the middle Eocene Contessa Highway section (Gubbio, Italy)," *Earth and Planetary Science Letters* 298(1–2):77–88, doi:10.1016/j.epsl.2010.07.027 (proposes upper C19n as the Lutetian/Bartonian criterion).

#### Priabonian base (= base of upper Eocene) — 37.8 Ma (or 37.7) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Alano, Piave, Italy · base of the Tiziano crystal tuff bed / LAD of large acarininids
- **Ratification:** Agnini et al. (2020) *Episodes* 44(2):151–173
- **Discussion:** A well-constrained boundary using a radiometrically datable tuff bed as the primary marker, with biostratigraphy, magnetostratigraphy, and radiometric ages mutually cross-checked.

#### Rupelian base (= base of Oligocene = E/O boundary) — 33.9 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Massignano, Ancona, Italy · extinction (LAD) of the planktonic foraminifera *Hantkenina* / *Cribrohantkenina*
- **Ratification:** Premoli Silva & Jenkins (1993) *Episodes* 16:379–382
- **Discussion:** The Eocene-Oligocene transition corresponds to the Oi-1 cooling and expansion of the Antarctic ice sheet, with the extinction of *Hantkenina* serving as a globally traceable marker.

#### Chattian base — 27.8 Ma (or 28.1) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Monte Cagnero, Central Apennines, Italy · *Chiloguembelina cubensis* HCO (highest common occurrence) / base of chron C9n
- **Ratification:** Coccioni et al. (2018) *Episodes* 41(1):17–32
- **Discussion:** A microfossil-abundance datum combined with magnetostratigraphy and astronomical tuning. The difference between the published age (27.8) and the magnetochronology-based value (28.1) reflects differences in age model and tuning.

### Neogene System

> The system that best exemplifies the essence of astronomical tuning based on Mediterranean cyclostratigraphy. Sapropel and calcareous cycles are matched bed by bed to Milankovitch orbital forcing, so most boundaries are calibrated to the astronomical time scale (ATNTS) at the kyr level. Two series: Miocene, Pliocene. (Pleistocene and Quaternary are treated separately.)

#### Aquitanian base (= base of Neogene and Miocene = P/N boundary) — 23.03 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Lemme-Carrosio, Italy · base of magnetic chron C6Cn.2n
- **Ratification:** Steininger et al. (1997) *Episodes* 20:23–28
- **Discussion:** The Paleogene/Neogene boundary is defined by a magnetic reversal, later tuned to 23.03 Ma via Mediterranean cyclostratigraphy as the lower anchor of the ATNTS.
- **Supporting papers:** *astronomical framework* Lourens, Hilgen, Shackleton, Laskar & Wilson (2004) "The Neogene Period," in *A Geologic Time Scale 2004*, Cambridge UP (ATNTS). *orbital solution* Laskar et al. (2004) *Astronomy & Astrophysics* 428:261–285 (La2004).

#### Burdigalian base — ~20.44 Ma · undefined · pending
- **Definition:** candidate Mediterranean sections · near the FAD of *Globigerinoides altiaperturus* / upper C6An (the current ~20.44 Ma is a magnetostratigraphic calibration value)
- **Ratification:** undefined. Still unratified as of 2025, with candidates including St. Thomas (Malta), Contessa (Italy), and the Eastern Alps (Austria).
- **Discussion:** The only ordinary boundary in the Neogene without a defined GSSP.
- **Supporting papers:** Fabbrini, Baldassini, Caricchi, Foresi et al. (2019) "In search of the Burdigalian GSSP: new evidence from the Contessa Section (Italy)," *Italian Journal of Geosciences* 138(2):274–295, doi:10.3301/IJG.2019.07.

#### Langhian base (= base of middle Miocene) — 15.98 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** "Lower La Vedova Beach," Ancona, Italy (17.84 m level, midpoint of the dark marly interval above Megabed IV) · astronomical tuning (≈100-kyr eccentricity maximum), secondarily near the FAD of *Praeorbulina glomerosa*
- **Ratification:** Turco et al. (2023, online) *Episodes* 47(2):311–333. Astronomical age 15.981 Ma (La2004) / 15.978 Ma (La2011)
- **Discussion:** The long-undefined Langhian base was fixed by astronomical tuning of a cyclic section. Because the marker is not a biostratigraphic event but an eccentricity-maximum tuning level, it is emblematic of Neogene astronomical tuning.

#### Serravallian base — 13.82 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Ras il Pellegrin, Malta · boundary between the Globigerina Limestone and Blue Clay Fm (near the LAD of *Sphenolithus heteromorphus*)
- **Ratification:** Hilgen et al. (2009) *Episodes* 32:152–166
- **Discussion:** A lithologic boundary anchored to astronomically tuned cycles. Corresponds to the cooling (Mi events) following the Middle Miocene Climatic Optimum.

#### Tortonian base (= base of upper Miocene) — 11.63 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Monte dei Corvi, Ancona, Italy · astronomical tuning, midpoint of sapropel cycle 76 (*Discoaster kugleri* LCO)
- **Ratification:** Hilgen et al. (2005) *Episodes* 28:6–17
- **Discussion:** A model of Mediterranean cyclostratigraphic astronomical chronology, matching sapropel cycles to orbital forcing one bed at a time.

#### Messinian base — 7.246 Ma (or 7.251) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Oued Akrech, Rabat, Morocco · FRO (first regular occurrence) of the planktonic foraminifera *Globorotalia miotumida*
- **Ratification:** Hilgen et al. (2000) *Episodes* 23:172–178
- **Discussion:** Note that the Messinian Salinity Crisis (MSC) is an internal event (after ~5.97 Ma), not the base of this stage. The boundary is defined by an Atlantic–Mediterranean gateway biostratigraphic event and astronomical tuning.

#### Zanclean base (= base of Pliocene = M/P boundary) — 5.333 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Eraclea Minoa, Sicily · base of the Trubi Fm / upper chron C3r (re-flooding after the end of the salinity crisis)
- **Ratification:** Van Couvering et al. (2000) *Episodes* 23:179–187
- **Discussion:** The boundary is the base of the Trubi marls, deposited as the Mediterranean was recharged with Atlantic seawater after the MSC, recording the dramatic end of the crisis. Tuned by cyclostratigraphy to 5.333 Ma.

#### Piacenzian base — 3.6 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Punta Piccola, Sicily · base of chron C2An (Gauss) / carbonate cycle 77
- **Ratification:** Castradori et al. (1998) *Episodes* 21:88–93
- **Discussion:** Defined by the base of the Gauss normal-polarity chron and an astronomically tuned carbonate cycle. Around this time, a harbinger of intensifying Northern Hemisphere glaciation.

> **Note:** The Gelasian, formerly the uppermost stage of the Neogene and Pliocene, was moved to the Quaternary/Pleistocene by a 2009 decision. Therefore the current uppermost boundary of the Neogene is the base of the Gelasian (2.58 Ma, under the purview of the Quaternary), discussed below.

### Quaternary System

> The era we currently live in, comprising two epochs, the Pleistocene and Holocene. A 2009 IUGS ratification moved the Gelasian from the Pliocene down into the lower Pleistocene, lowering the base of the Quaternary from 1.8 to 2.58 Ma (a decision meant to capture the onset of large-scale Northern Hemisphere glaciation in the base, and one that was accompanied by controversy). Its defining markers are also distinctive: the lower part is tied to geomagnetic reversals and sapropels, but the three Holocene boundaries are the first ever defined by non-outcrop records such as Greenland ice cores and an Indian stalagmite.

#### Gelasian base (= base of the Quaternary and Pleistocene = Neogene/Quaternary boundary) — 2.58 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Monte San Nicola, Gela, Sicily · base of the Matuyama reversed chron (C2r), just above sapropel MPRS 250
- **Ratification:** original Gelasian GSSP Rio et al. (1998); redefined as the base of the Quaternary and Pleistocene by 2009 IUGS ratification — Gibbard et al. (2010) *Episodes* 33:152–158
- **Discussion:** originally the base of the Gelasian within the Pliocene, it was elevated in 2009 to the official base of the Quaternary and Pleistocene. The base of the Quaternary dropped from 1.8 to 2.58 Ma, putting an end to the debate over "where to start the Quaternary" (critical views still persist).

#### Calabrian base — 1.80 Ma · GSSP
- **Definition:** Vrica, Calabria, Italy · just above sapropel Bed 'e', at the top of the Olduvai normal-polarity chron (C2n)
- **Ratification:** Cita et al. (2012) *Episodes* 35:388–397
- **Discussion:** Vrica is the historic site that was the original Pliocene/Pleistocene boundary GSSP in 1985. When the base of the Pleistocene dropped to the Gelasian (2.58 Ma) in 2009, this site was redefined as the base of the lower Pleistocene (Calabrian) — a prime example of the same physical point having its definitional rank changed.

#### Chibanian base (= base of the Middle Pleistocene) — 0.774 Ma (774.1 ka) · GSSP
- **Definition:** Chiba, Japan · Matuyama–Brunhes geomagnetic reversal, with the Byk-E tephra 1.1 m below it
- **Ratification:** Suganuma et al. (2021) *Episodes* 44(3):317–347 (ratified 2020-01-17)
- **Discussion:** the first geologic time unit named after a locality in Japan. Astronomical tuning yields a reversal age of 774.1 ka. The M–B boundary is an excellent global correlation marker, and the Byk-E tephra just below the reversal surface fixes the GSSP point.

#### Upper (Late) Pleistocene (Tarantian) base — provisional ~129 ka · pending
- **Definition:** candidate base of the Last Interglacial (Eemian), approximating MIS 5e (~130 ka) · candidate outcrop Fronte, Taranto, Italy
- **Ratification:** undefined. The Upper Pleistocene GSSP working group was newly established 2025-05-15 (chair A. Amorosi). A proposal to the SQS is planned after candidate review and voting.
- **Discussion:** the only undefined regular boundary within the Quaternary. There is consensus on tying the boundary to the onset of the last interglacial, but a fundamental difficulty is that this event is time-transgressive, making a single-outcrop isochronous definition hard.
- **Supporting papers:** Negri, Amorosi, Antonioli et al. (2015) "A potential global boundary stratotype section and point (GSSP) for the Tarentian Stage, Upper Pleistocene, from the Taranto area (Italy)," *Quaternary International* 383:145–157, doi:10.1016/j.quaint.2014.08.057.

#### Greenlandian base (= base of the Holocene = Pleistocene/Holocene boundary) — 11,700 yr b2k (0.0117 Ma) · GSSP
- **Definition:** NGRIP2 ice core, Greenland · end of the Younger Dryas, abrupt shift in deuterium excess
- **Ratification:** original definition Walker et al. (2009) *J. Quaternary Science* 24(1):3–17; reaffirmed along with the three-fold subdivision by Walker et al. (2018) *Episodes* 41(4):213–223
- **Discussion:** the first case of adopting an ice core rather than an outcrop as the GSSP medium, and a distinctive definition that pins the age to an absolute count-based age (b2k) from annual layer counting. It is fixed at 11,700 b2k on the GICC05 timescale.
- **Supporting papers:** *timescale* Rasmussen et al. (2006) *J. Geophys. Res.* 111:D06102 (YD end 11,703 b2k); Vinther et al. (2006) *JGR* 111:D13102.

#### Northgrippian base (= base of the Middle Holocene) — 8,236 yr b2k · GSSP
- **Definition:** NGRIP1 ice core, Greenland · the 8.2 ka cold event (freshwater input from the collapse of the Laurentide Ice Sheet)
- **Ratification:** Walker et al. (2018) *Episodes* 41(4):213–223
- **Discussion:** newly established with the three-fold division of the Holocene in 2018. It takes the most abrupt cold event of the Holocene as its marker.

#### Meghalayan base (= base of the Upper Holocene) — 4,250 yr b2k · GSSP
- **Definition:** Mawmluh Cave stalagmite, Meghalaya, India · the 4.2 ka aridification event
- **Ratification:** Walker et al. (2018) *Episodes* 41(4):213–223
- **Discussion:** the first case of using a stalagmite as the GSSP medium. It adopts as its marker the 4.2 ka mega-drought event detected across several continents (discussed in connection with the collapse of early civilizations). This is the interval we currently belong to. There are also dissenting views on the global synchroneity of the marker event.

### Anthropocene (proposed, not ratified)

> **Status:** not officially ratified; rejected in March 2024 and not included in the geologic time scale. The Anthropocene Working Group (AWG) proposed placing human-driven Earth-system change as a separate epoch/series, with a GSSP candidate corresponding to the ~1950s "Great Acceleration" at **Crawford Lake** in Ontario, Canada (markers: nuclear-test-derived plutonium fallout and fly-ash).

**How it was rejected:** In a March 2024 vote of the Subcommission on Quaternary Stratigraphy (SQS), the Crawford Lake proposal was rejected with 4 in favor, 12 against, and 3 abstentions. The AWG lodged an objection, but the ICS executive upheld the validity of the vote, and the chairs of the IUGS subcommissions supported it nearly unanimously. In an official statement on 2024-03-20, IUGS confirmed the decision not to formalize the Anthropocene as an epoch.

**2025–2026 developments:** The concept has persisted even after the rejection. The framework of the **"Anthropocene as an event, not an epoch"** raised by Gibbard and others (an informal, non-stratigraphic concept encompassing the time-transgressive human impact spanning tens of thousands of years) has emerged as an alternative and was still under discussion in 2025. However, as of 2025–2026 it has not been granted the status of an official GSSP-based stratigraphic unit.

**Supporting papers:**
- candidate outcrop and reference sections — Waters, Turner, Zalasiewicz & Head (2023) "Candidate sites and other reference sections for the GSSP of the Anthropocene series" *The Anthropocene Review* (main Crawford Lake proposal).
- alternative concept — Gibbard et al. (2022) "The Anthropocene as an Event, not an Epoch" *J. Quaternary Science* 37(3):395–399.
- decision document — IUGS statement (2024-03-20).

## 5. Accuracy caveats

- **Age values:** mostly based on GTS2020. ICS continues to issue revisions in 2022 (Cambrian base 538.8 Ma) and 2024/12 (Ordovician, etc.), so values may differ between sources (e.g., the coexistence of 190.8 vs 192.9 Ma for several Jurassic boundaries).
- **Bibliographic accuracy:** the supporting bibliography was cross-checked against CrossRef/publisher records in 2026-07 (authors, volume/page, DOI confirmed). Corrections include: the Asselian age model (Ramezani et al. 2007) is in *EPSL* 256:244–257, not *American Journal of Science*; the Roadian/Wordian GSSP is in *Permophiles* 34:3–11, not *Episodes*; Coniacian is *Episodes* 45(2) (← 42(2)) and Campanian is 46(3) (← 46(2)); Dapingian is the 2009 *Episodes* 32(2):96–113 paper (2005 was the proposal); the Valanginian candidate-section paper is Kenjo et al. 2021. **Two items remain without a formal paper**: the Eoarchean *Episodes* ratification (forthcoming, unpublished) and the Aeronian *Episodes* ratification (unpublished) — confirmed via ICS chart v2023/09 and the IUGS/ICS announcement respectively.
- **Time-sensitivity:** GSSP status keeps changing. Valanginian had conflicting statuses across sources at the time of survey (ratified 2024-12), and Berriasian, Aptian, Bartonian, Burdigalian, Upper Pleistocene, and Cryogenian remain undefined as of 2026-07. The Anthropocene was rejected in 2024.
- **Prioritize the primary catalog:** this document is a structured entry point. Individual boundary citations should be verified against the **ICS Table of GSSPs** (which records the ratification citation for each boundary) and the **GTS2020** original text.
