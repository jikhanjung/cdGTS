# Key GSSP·GSSA Papers

*English · [한국어](gssp-gssa-key-papers.md)*

> Status: **Reference-literature survey.** Across ratified and under-discussion GSSP (Global Boundary
> Stratotype Section and Point) and GSSA (Global Standard Stratigraphic Age) boundaries, this collects key
> papers spanning three categories — (1) boundary-defining data, (2) age model (radiometric / astronomical
> calibration), (3) formal ratification notices — via multi-source web research + adversarial verification
> (a claim is killed if 2 of 3 votes refute it). Surveyed 2026-07. **Note: this list is illustrative, not
> exhaustive** — it does not enumerate a per-stage paper for every Phanerozoic boundary. The complete
> per-boundary bibliography already exists in the ICS/GTSF tables of §1 (with individual Episodes/Lethaia
> citations); only a subset was independently verified here.

Related: [case-precambrian-gssa_en.md](case-precambrian-gssa_en.md) · [case-permian-triassic_en.md](case-permian-triassic_en.md) · [case-cambrian-base-correlation_en.md](case-cambrian-base-correlation_en.md) · [competing-models_en.md](competing-models_en.md)

## 0. The three categories

| Category | Meaning | cdGTS mapping |
|---|---|---|
| **(1) Boundary-defining data** | The marker that physically fixes the boundary — fossil FAD, paleomagnetic reversal, isotope excursion, etc. | data node · correlation event |
| **(2) Age model** | Basis for the boundary age — radiometric (U-Pb, etc.), astronomical (astrochronology), orbital tuning | process node · age determination |
| **(3) Ratification notice** | Official IUGS/ICS ratification announcement — usually in *Episodes* | release node · ratified release |

A single paper often covers all three (e.g., Walker 2018, Suganuma 2021).

## 2. Verified case papers (detail by category)

Each entry passed 3-vote adversarial verification (3-0 unanimous unless noted).

### 2.1 Cenozoic — Quaternary

**Walker, M. et al. (2018)** — *Formal ratification of the subdivision of the Holocene Series/Epoch...*
*Episodes* **41(4): 213-223.** DOI 10.18814/epiiugs/2018/018016
- **Boundary:** subdivides the Holocene into 3 stages — Greenlandian · Northgrippian · Meghalayan
- **Categories (1)+(2)+(3) all.** Northgrippian GSSP = NGRIP1 Greenland ice core (8236 yr b2k); Meghalayan
  GSSP = speleothem, Mawmluh Cave, NE India (4250 yr b2k); Greenlandian base reuses the existing Holocene
  GSSP (NGRIP2, 11,700 yr b2k)
- Ratified by IUGS EC 2018-06-14. *(Core data/ages/notice 3-0; only the opening wording was 2-1 — "two new
  GSSPs + one reused" is the accurate framing)*

**Suganuma, Y., Okada, M., Head, M.J., Kameo, K. et al. (2021)** — Chibanian Stage / Middle Pleistocene Subseries GSSP
*Episodes* **44(3): 317-347.** DOI 10.18814/epiiugs/2020/020080
- **Boundary:** base of Chibanian (Middle Pleistocene), Chiba Section, Japan. Ratified by IUGS EC 2020-01-17
- **Category (1):** primary marker = Matuyama–Brunhes paleomagnetic reversal; golden spike 1.1 m below the
  reversal midpoint, at the base of the Ontake-Byakubi-E (Byk-E) tephra
- **Category (2):** astronomical / orbital-MIS tuning → 774.1 ka for the GSSP position, 772.9 ka for the M–B
  reversal (cross-checked by a U-Pb zircon age of the Byk-E tephra)
- **Category (3):** formal ratification notice

### 2.2 Paleozoic — Permian (redefinition case)

**Shen, S.-Z. et al. (2024)** — **redefinition** of the Wuchiapingian Stage base GSSP
*Episodes* **47(1): 147-177.** DOI 10.18814/epiiugs/2023/023023
- **Boundary:** base of Wuchiapingian (Lopingian, Permian). The originally ratified (2004/2005) section was
  permanently flooded in 2020, so a new excavated GSSP at Penglaitan + a new SABS (Standard Auxiliary
  Boundary Stratotype) at Fengshan were designated
- **Category (1):** defined by the FAD of conodont *Clarkina postbitteri* (lineage *Jinogondolella granti*
  → *C. postbitteri* → *C. dukouensis*). *(canonical marker is the subspecies C. p. postbitteri; the source
  wording is at species level)*
- **Category (2):** constrained by U-Pb 259.51 ± 0.21 Ma from the uppermost Emeishan basalt
- **Significance:** a **GSSP redefinition/relocation** case — shows the golden spike can move when a section
  becomes inaccessible or new data appear

### 2.3 Precambrian — GSSA → rock-based transition

**Shields-Zhou, G., Porter, S., Halverson, G. (2016)** — *A new rock-based definition for the Cryogenian Period (circa 720–635 Ma)*
*Episodes* **39(1).**
- **Boundary:** base of Cryogenian. Originally fixed **numerically at 850 Ma by a GSSA (1988)** → invalidated
  because widespread (Sturtian) glaciation actually began ~717 Ma. The new GSSP is to sit at a globally
  correlative level just below the first appearance of widespread glaciation, with an interim "calibrated
  age" of ~720 Ma
- **Categories (1)+(2):** defining data (glaciation-onset level) + age basis (calibrated age)

**Shields, G. et al. (2018)** — *Descent into the Cryogenian*
*Precambrian Research* (S0301926818304054)
- **Status:** **no consensus** on best section/criteria; base still chronometrically defined (~720 Ma),
  **GSSP pending ratification**
- *(Caution: the claim "GSSA removed and formally replaced by a GSSP" was **refuted 1-2** in verification.
  ICS removed the GSSA and replaced it with an interim calibrated numerical age; no GSSP yet. The specific
  "Cryogenian 717–635.5 Ma" anchor was also **refuted 0-3**.)*

**Shields, G. et al. (2022)** — *A template for an improved rock-based subdivision of the pre-Cryogenian timescale*
*J. Geol. Soc.* **179(1),** jgs2020-222
- **Aim:** the pre-Cryogenian (pre-720 Ma) is currently subdivided by **GSSA-style rounded numerical ages**,
  causing mismatches between names and actual geology. Proposes rock-based/event-based concepts and
  provisional approximate ages to accelerate GSSA removal (**a proposal, not ratified**)
- **Proposed new names:** Proterozoic Eon as Paleo- (Skourian·Rhyacian·Orosirian) / Meso- (Statherian·
  Calymmian·Ectasian·Stenian) / Neo- (Kleisian/pre-Tonian·Tonian·Cryogenian·Ediacaran). **Skourian** and
  **Kleisian** are new period names
- *(Core claims 3-0; only the "accelerate GSSA removal" framing was 2-1)*

### 2.4 Age model / astrochronology (Cenozoic)

**Zeebe, R.E. & Lourens, L.J. (2019)** — *Solar System chaos and the Paleocene–Eocene boundary age...*
*Science* **365(6456): 926-929.** DOI 10.1126/science.aax0612
- **Boundary:** Paleocene–Eocene (Paleogene)
- **Category (2) exemplar:** a new astronomical (orbital) solution **ZB18a** provides absolute
  astrochronology back to ~58 Ma; combined with geological data it yields a P–E boundary age of
  **56.01 ± 0.05 Ma**
- **Caution:** this is an **author-derived value**, not an ICS-ratified age — an illustration of the
  radiometric/astronomical dating basis

### 2.5 Methodology (defense of GSSP)

**Harper, D.A.T., Angiolini, L., Henderson, C.M., Servais, T. (2026; online 2025-12)** —
*Stability and precision in chronostratigraphic definition: The GSSP is the solution*
*Palaeogeography, Palaeoclimatology, Palaeoecology.* DOI 10.1016/j.palaeo.2025.113515
- **Content:** argues the GSSP approach — defining each boundary by a fixed "golden spike" in a rock
  succession coinciding with biological/stratigraphic markers — is the best way to define and correlate
  geological time. Rebuts numerical/chronometric-only alternatives ("Imprecision and instability of the
  Phanerozoic chronostratigraphic scale")
- Explicitly scopes GSSP (Phanerozoic) separately from GSSA (numerical Precambrian definition)

## 4. Verification caveats

- Coverage is illustrative — verified cases give strong evidence for specific boundaries (Holocene ·
  Chibanian · Wuchiapingian · Cryogenian · pre-Cryogenian · Paleocene–Eocene) + 2 reference catalogs, but not
  a per-stage paper list for every Phanerozoic GSSP
- Time-sensitive: the Cryogenian base and all pre-Cryogenian subdivisions remain **unresolved/unratified** as
  of 2026. The ~720 Ma value is interim; the Shields et al. (2022) Proterozoic naming (Skourian, Kleisian,
  etc.) is a **proposal**, not ratified ICS nomenclature
- Terminology: since 2015 the Cryogenian base is strictly not a GSSA but an **"interim calibrated numerical
  age"** after ICS removed the 850 Ma GSSA
- GTS2020 (Gradstein et al.) was confirmed only as the basis for GTSF ages — this flagship reference should be
  **consulted directly**
- One primary source (Lyell Collection jgs2020-222) failed direct fetch (HTTP 403), confirmed only via
  abstract/eprint
- Two Cryogenian-detail claims were **refuted and excluded** (see §2.3 notes)
